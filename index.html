The Challenge

Councils Are Doing the Work.
Residents Don't Always See It.

Teams are small. Budgets are tight. Time is limited. And while AI and digital tools have accelerated communication, they don’t replace strategy, experience, or a nuanced understanding of how local areas actually function — how decisions are made, who influences them, and what matters to the communities they serve.

📬

Low Engagement

Newsletters go unread. Social posts get ignored. Residents feel disconnected from council decisions that affect their daily lives.

🗳️

Consultation Fatigue

Public consultations struggle to attract meaningful responses. Without structured communications, important voices go unheard.

🏛️

Councillor Recruitment

Attracting new councillors is harder than ever. The role is poorly understood and rarely promoted effectively to potential candidates.

📢

Major Projects

When big changes happen — development, devolution, restructuring — councils need calm, clear communication. Most aren't equipped for it.

📊

No Strategy

Without a communications strategy, efforts are reactive, inconsistent, and impossible to measure or improve over time.

🤝

Limited Partnerships

Local businesses and organisations could be powerful allies — but building those relationships takes time and a strategic approach.
